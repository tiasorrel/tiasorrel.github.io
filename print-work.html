Print Work

This is a collection of books, posters, and magazine spreads that I have designed for various classes and organizations. Although I prefer digital design, I see print design as a great exercise in fundamental aspects of design, such as typography, color, and layout.

A Streetcar Named Desire

In my Graphic Design 1 class, I designed the poster for the play A Streetcar Named Desire by Tennessee Williams. My goal was to convey aggression. I photographed a hand crumpling a piece of paper with the name of the play written on it to depict force. I used red as a background to enforce the feeling of both passion and violence.

Pentagram Magazine Spread

I created this magazine spread for a Typography 1 Tools assignment. I took an existing article about the renowned design consultancy Pentagram and created a clean and modern layout for it, reflecting much of what the article was saying about the light and studious atmosphere of the Pentagram London studio.

Book Cover Series

The goal for this Typography 2 project was to define a system using typography, format, and color to create a unified identity for the Canadian Center for Architecture. We were to implement this system into the book covers of three architects whose works represent three distinct styles and movements: Louis Sullivan (Chicago style, influenced by Art Nouveau); Richard Neutra (Modernism); Frank Gehry (Deconstructivism).

My designs feature abstracted, color-blocked versions of one of each of the architects' famous buildings: Sullivan's Carson, Pirie, Scott and Company Building in Chicago, Gehry's Walt Disney Concert Hall in Los Angeles, and Neutra's Kauffman House in Palm Springs, California.
